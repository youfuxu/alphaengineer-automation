Bank balance vs net worth

Bank balance = cash you have right now. It resets every month. It doesn't show your investments, your debt, or your real financial trajectory.

Net worth = all assets − all liabilities. It compounds. It shows whether you're actually getting wealthier or just earning-and-spending at a higher level.

Same salary, two engineers. One has a $150K net worth at 35. The other has $15K. The bank balance doesn't tell you which you are.

The monthly ritual that works

  • 1.First of every month: log in to each account and record current balance
  • 2.Total assets − total liabilities = net worth for this month
  • 3.Track the month-over-month change (not just the total)
  • 4.Ask: "What moved the number this month? What can I improve next month?"

Takes 15 minutes. Gives you more financial clarity than daily bank balance checking.

The motivation effect

  • →Checking bank balance: anxiety (payday coming? rent due?)
  • →Checking net worth: progress (it's up $2K from last month)
  • →You can't control the bank balance directly. You control the net worth.
  • →Seeing net worth compound is the thing that makes people actually keep investing.

Behavior follows measurement. Measure the right thing.

Tools that make it easy
